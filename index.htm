Jesus' Unfulfilled Prophecy

Jesus' Unfulfilled Prophecy?

By Iesus Church | April fifteenth, Anno Domini MMXXIII

esus often said throughout His ministry that we would be persecuted for His sake, that men will hate them and even kill them for righteousness' sake (2 Timothy 3:12, John 15:19, 2 Corinthians 4:8, Matthew 8:20). Hering that in church, undoubtedly some have wondered, What persecution? For indeed, we sit in our pews without need for discretion. We wear crucifixes and receive no objection. There are Christians all around us, and in no wise are we persecuted. So, then, was Jesus' prophecy false? Is this Jesus' Unfulfilled Prophecy?

In no wise! As Christians we know the Word of God is inerrant, and that the teachings of Jesus Christ are true. Therefore we realize this: we are doing something wrong. Christians are doing something wrong. How come we are not being persecuted? If we are followers of Christ, why do we have a place to lay our heads?

Behold, the answer is this: we should not be Christians. I am not a Christian. We should be followers of Christ. Christians have a place to lay their heads. Christians are fat and wealthy. Christians are indolent and vapid; they dare not wander past their comfortable ventillated churches, to stand up from their cushioned pews. These are the Christians which Christ knoweth not (Matthew 25:12, Luke 13:25). Rather, the Follower of Christ is hated. The follower of Christ has no where to lay his head. The follower of Christ takes action (Matthew 21:12). And the follower of Christ rises up against the evildoers (Psalms 94:16).

Now, what is this I am saying in practical terms? Come with me, and let us take a stance against homosexuality (Leviticus 18:22). Let us stand against the rosary (Matthew 6:7). Let us stand against feminism (1 Timothy 2:12). Let us stand against miscegenation (Ezra 10:10, Nehemiah 13:27, 1 Esdras 9:7). The world will hate us therefore, but God will reward us. This is what Jesus prophecized about: that the world would persecute us for His sake. They will kill us thinking we are hateful, but this is what Jesus prophecized about (John 16:2). Thus you will forget what it is to be a Christian, and will understand what it is to be a follower of Christ and the true plight thereof. Get off your pews, we have work to do.
